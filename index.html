WHY SHOULD I PATENT MY INNOVATION

 

 

 

①

If you are an Individual, just like any other asset transferred to your kins, Patent also belongs to your natural heir.

 

 

②

If you are part of a corporate or institutions, Patent gives you stronger market position.

 

 

③

If global reach is your ambition, Patent provides wings. But its up to the inventor  to flap

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

ANY CLASSIC EXAMPLE OF WHY I/WE SHOULDN’T PATENT?

 

①

Sir Tim Berners-Lee did not go for his invention world wide web (The internet). He said “If I had tried to demand fees…there would be no World Wide Web. There would be lots of small webs.”

 

②

Kalashnikov did not go for patent, but the company which manufactured AK 47 went for patent. He said “I created it for the good of his country and wanted no profit off it

 

③

Chemist John Walker did not patent Match sticks. He lived around 1820 and anyone could only imagine how much wealth his generation will enjoy if patented. But generously dedicated his invention to society.
